ABOUT

Chandigarh is a city and union territory in India that serves as the capital of Punjab and Haryana. It is known for its modern architecture, urban planning, and green spaces.

After the partition of India in 1947, the Government decided to build a new planned city. It was developed in the early 1950s and designed by French architect Le Corbusier. Chandigarh is India’s first planned city, known for its sectors, clean roads, and greenery.

Today, Chandigarh is recognized nationwide as a modern city that blends architecture with greenery.



TOURIST PLACES

Rock Garden: A sculpture garden by Nek Chand, with thousands of figures made from recycled materials.

Rose Garden: Asia's largest rose garden with over 1,600 varieties.

Sukhna Lake: A man-made reservoir at the Shivalik foothills, created in 1958. Popular for boating and walks.

Japanese Garden: Features Japanese-style architecture, pagodas and ponds.

Bird Park: A nature park with many bird species.
